The basic physics of airborne FDEM is illustrated by the animation below. Click through the radio buttons to see how an airborne FDEM system senses a buried conductive object.

A helicopter tows a tube containing a pair of transmitter-receiver loops.
2D The transmitter loop carries a time-harmonic current that generates a time-harmonic primary magnetic field everywhere.
The time-harmonic primary magnetic field induced eddy currents in the conductive object below the surface.
The induced current in the object generates a secondary magnetic field, which is measured by the receiver loop.
